This is a 62-year-old female patient who was relatively healthy before. 
  The patient had been well until 3 days ago, generalized yellowish discoloration over whole body developed. The associated symptoms included retro-sternal colicky pain, clay color stool for 1 day, back soreness and headache. The pain was intermittent(up to 1 hr at each episode), without precipitating or exacerbating factors and would resolve spontaneously. In addition, poor appetite and weight loss of 8 kg(60->52kg) in 6 months were also noted. 
  The patient denied fever, diarrhea, hepatitis virus carrier history before. Due to the above symptoms, she was brought to our ED on 2015/09/28.
  On presentation, her vital signs were stable. Physical exams revealed icteric appearnce over sclerae and whole body surface. Liver function tests revealed cholestasis pattern with direct hyperbilirubinemia. CT exams on 9/28 revealed gastric antrum wall swelling with bile duct, right ureter, and peritoneal involvement. Malignancy was suspected. Under the impression of gastric antrum tumor with bile duct, right ureter obstruction and suspected peritoneal carcinomatosis, she was admitted for further evaluation and management.
